# Airline-Revenue-Management-Pricing-Analytics

## Overview

This project analyzes airline pricing behavior using SQL, Python, and Power BI to simulate real-world Revenue Management activities. The analysis focuses on fare escalation patterns, booking-window dynamics, competitor pricing strategies, and route-level revenue opportunities.

---

## Objectives

- Analyze how fares change as departure approaches.
- Evaluate pricing differences across airlines and travel classes.
- Identify premium routes with strong pricing power.
- Generate insights that support pricing and revenue optimization decisions.

---

## Tools & Technologies

### SQL (PostgreSQL)
- Data cleaning and transformation
- Route creation
- Booking-window segmentation
- Pricing analysis

### Python (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib)
- Fare trend analysis
- Business insights generation
- Data visualization

### Power BI
- Interactive Revenue Management dashboard
- KPI monitoring and performance tracking

---

## Key Analyses

### Booking Window Analytics

- Created booking windows based on days before departure.
- Analyzed fare escalation patterns across the booking horizon.
- Identified significant fare increases during the final booking period.

### Competitor Pricing Analysis

- Compared average fares across major airlines.
- Evaluated airline pricing strategies and market positioning.

### Route Opportunity Analysis

- Identified premium routes based on average fare performance.
- Compared Business and Economy Class pricing across routes.

## Key Insights

- Last-minute bookings generated the highest average fares.
- Average fares increased significantly as departure approached.
- Vistara maintained the highest average fare among competing airlines.
- Chennai–Bangalore and Kolkata–Chennai emerged as premium routes.
- Business Class consistently commanded a substantial fare premium over Economy Class.

Developed a Revenue Management Analytics solution that transforms airline fare data into actionable pricing insights. The project supports:
